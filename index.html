One Small Stud for Man
Image Credit: Me, Myself, and I

Explanation: Using only 4KB of RAM and a 32KB hard disk, scientists at NASA put people on the moon. Commander Neil Armstrong became the first person to step on the moon on July 21st, 1969 on the spaceflight Apollo 11. Lunar Module Pilot Buzz Aldrin followed him onto the lunar surface where they spent two hours and thirty-two minutes exploring Tranquillity Base , the area they landed in. On the Moon, the two astronauts collected samples, conducted experiments, and captured iconic photographs . The mission took 8 days, 3 hours, and 18 minutes in total. They left two memorial medals, a gold replica of an olive branch, and a disk containing messages of world leaders behind on the Moon. While the last Apollo mission, Apollo 17, was flown in 1972, there are still ongoing spaceflights to the Moon. This diorama shows Buzz Aldrin planting the American flag into the lunar surface, where it still stands today.
